Featured Research Project

A more detailed summary of my current WASP-39b exoplanet atmosphere work.

WASP-39b: Independent NIR Transmission Spectrum Study

This project analyzes the archival HST/WFC3 transmission spectrum of WASP-39b across 0.82–1.63 μm using a Python-based workflow. The analysis reproduces the dominant water absorption feature and derives planetary and atmospheric properties directly from published photometry and system parameters.

The study is designed as an exercise in independent archival science: reproducing a published exoplanet atmosphere result while also extending it through first-principles physical calculations and clearer computational presentation.

Main Results

  • Gaussian fit to the dominant H₂O feature at 1.444 ± 0.011 μm
  • Feature amplitude of 2385 ± 278 ppm
  • Flat-spectrum test gives χ²red = 6.45, strongly rejecting a featureless atmosphere
  • Measured water feature corresponds to about 2.2 scale heights for a high-metallicity atmosphere
  • Derived physical properties include g = 4.26 m s⁻², Teq = 1117 K, and ρ = 0.167 g cm⁻³
