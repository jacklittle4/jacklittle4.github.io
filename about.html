About

Jack Little

Engineering Physics student. Computational physicist. Future graduate researcher in cosmology.

Background

The short version

I'm a third-year Engineering Physics student at Commonwealth University of Pennsylvania, Bloomsburg, with a minor in Mathematics. I'm a David J. Harper Scholar and serve as a Supplemental Instruction Leader for upper-division physics courses.

My academic work spans classical mechanics, electromagnetism, quantum mechanics, and increasingly, general relativity and cosmology. I do hands-on research in planetary science and have independent experience with observational astrophysics data pipelines.

Outside the classroom I build interactive physics simulations — not as a hobby exactly, but because I find that writing something from scratch forces a depth of understanding that coursework alone doesn't always provide. Everything on this site is written by hand in vanilla JavaScript.

Trajectory

Where I'm headed

I plan to pursue graduate study in theoretical and computational cosmology, with a particular focus on primordial black holes — their formation during the early universe, mass spectra under different inflationary scenarios, and their potential role as dark matter candidates.

The connection between PBH physics and dark matter is one of the most active and genuinely open problems in modern cosmology. Gravitational wave observations from LIGO/Virgo and future detectors like LISA are rapidly constraining the parameter space. I want to be working on that problem.

Between now and graduate school: finishing my undergraduate degree, deepening my mathematical background through topology and differential geometry, and building out this site as a working demonstration of what I can actually do computationally.
