Background

The short version.

I am a third-year Engineering Physics student at Commonwealth University of Pennsylvania, Bloomsburg, with a minor in Mathematics. I am a David J. Harper Scholar and serve as a Supplemental Instruction Leader for upper-division physics courses.

My coursework and projects span classical mechanics, electromagnetism, quantum mechanics, numerical methods, general relativity, and cosmology. I like building simulations because code turns a derivation into something testable.

This website is both a portfolio and a working notebook: a place where math, physics, and interface design meet in small experiments that can be pushed, broken, improved, and made clearer over time.

Trajectory

Where I am headed.

I plan to pursue graduate study in theoretical and computational cosmology, with a focus on primordial black holes, their mass spectra, and their potential role as dark matter candidates.

Between now and graduate school, I am deepening the math that supports that path: topology, differential geometry, numerical analysis, and the general relativity needed to reason about compact objects and gravitational-wave signatures.
