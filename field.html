Magnetic Field Explorer

This page visualizes the magnetic field around a long straight current-carrying wire. Instead of a simple static plot, this version lets you change the current strength, reverse the current direction, adjust field density, and show field lines and vectors in real time.

Controls

The wire is shown at the center. Use the right-hand rule to compare the direction of circulation.

What this shows

For a long straight wire, the magnetic field wraps in concentric circles around the wire. The direction depends on the current direction. If the current points out of the page, the field circulates counterclockwise; if the current points into the page, it circulates clockwise.

|B| ∝ I / r

Direction from the right-hand rule: thumb = current, curled fingers = magnetic field direction

This is a qualitative visualization, meant for exploration and presentation. It emphasizes field geometry and relative strength rather than exact SI scaling.
