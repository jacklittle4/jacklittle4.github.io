The mathematics

What is the Fourier Transform?

Any periodic signal can be expressed as a sum of sinusoids with different frequencies, amplitudes, and phases. The Fourier Transform computes how much of each frequency is present:

X(f) = ∫ x(t) · e^(−2πift) dt

For discrete signals (like digital audio), the Discrete Fourier Transform and its fast algorithm (FFT) achieve this in O(n log n) time.

Reading the spectrum

Interpreting the output

The waveform shows amplitude over time — what the signal "looks like". The spectrum shows power at each frequency — what the signal "contains".

A pure sine wave produces a single spike. A complex sound like speech or music produces a rich spectrum of harmonics. The FFT size controls the frequency resolution — larger FFTs resolve finer frequency differences but require more computation.
