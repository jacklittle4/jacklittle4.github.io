Schwarzschild metric

The geometry

The Schwarzschild metric describes spacetime curvature outside a spherically symmetric, non-rotating mass in geometric units (G = c = 1):

ds² = −(1 − rs/r) dt² + (1 − rs/r)−1 dr² + r² dΩ²

where rs = 2GM/c² is the Schwarzschild radius. The simulation works in the equatorial plane (θ = π/2), so dΩ² = dφ².

Equations of motion

The geodesic equations

Two conserved quantities arise from the metric's symmetries: energy E and angular momentum L. Setting b = L/E (impact parameter):

(dr/dλ)² = E² − (1 − rs/r)(L²/r² + ε)

where ε = 0 for photons (null geodesics) and ε = 1 for massive particles (timelike geodesics). Integrated via RK4 with adaptive step control near rs.
